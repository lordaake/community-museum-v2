Professor Sheila

Aeronautical Engineer visits!

10th of March 2023

Prof. Sheila Widnall is a renowned aeronautical engineer and a pioneer in the field of aerospace technology. With over four decades of experience in academia and research, she has made significant contributions to the development of aeronautics and has helped shape the future of space exploration. During her upcoming lectures, Prof. Widnall will be sharing her insights and expertise on the latest advancements in aeronautics, including the development of cutting-edge technologies and techniques that are revolutionizing the way we think about space travel.

In her first lecture, Prof. Widnall will delve into the history of aeronautics and explore some of the key milestones that have shaped our understanding of space and the universe. She will discuss the evolution of technology and the role that engineering has played in pushing the boundaries of what is possible.

In her second lecture, Prof. Widnall will focus on the present and future of aeronautics, providing a detailed overview of the latest innovations in space travel and the challenges that lie ahead. She will discuss the latest developments in propulsion systems, materials science, and other key areas of research, and will explore how these technologies are being used to push the boundaries of space exploration.

In her final lecture, Prof. Widnall will share her thoughts on the future of aeronautics and where she believes the field is headed in the coming years. She will discuss the impact of emerging technologies such as artificial intelligence, robotics, and advanced materials, and will explore how these technologies will shape the future of space travel. Overall, these lectures promise to be an exciting and informative event for anyone interested in aeronautics and the future of space exploration. Whether you are a student, researcher, or simply someone with a passion for science and technology, Prof. Widnall's lectures are not to be missed!
Night in the museum
Museum Sleepover!
24th of March 2023
The museum sleepover event promises to be a night filled with adventure, exploration, and discovery. As you arrive at the museum, you will be greeted by expert guides who will lead you on a behind-the-scenes tour of the museum's exhibits. You will have the opportunity to explore the exhibits at your own pace, learn about the fascinating stories behind each display, and get up close and personal with some of the most awe-inspiring artifacts in the museum's collection.

As the night falls, you will make your way to the sleeping area, where you will lay out your sleeping bags and settle in for the night. The museum will be transformed into a cozy campsite, complete with lanterns, sleeping mats, and all the amenities you need to make your stay comfortable.

But the night is far from over - as you drift off to sleep, you will be lulled by the sounds of the museum's inhabitants. The exhibits come to life after dark, and you might catch a glimpse of the dinosaurs roaming the halls or the space shuttle blasting off into the cosmos.

In the morning, you will wake up to a delicious breakfast served in the museum's café, before heading back into the exhibits for another round of exploration and discovery. This is an experience that your family will never forget - a chance to get up close and personal with the wonders of science and the natural world, and to make memories that will last a lifetime. So pack your sleeping bags, grab your sense of adventure, and get ready to experience the magic of the museum sleepover!
Energetica Exhibition on Loan
Explore the power of elements! 15th of May 2023
The Energetica exhibition is not to be missed - it is a fascinating, interactive experience that explores the power of the elements and how we can harness them to create sustainable sources of energy. On loan from the NEMO Science Museum in Amsterdam, this exhibition has been a hit with audiences around the world, and now it is coming to the Community Science Museum for a limited time.

One of the highlights of the exhibition is the "Wind Island" installation, which showcases the incredible power of wind and how it can be harnessed to generate energy. Visitors will be able to see how turbines work to capture the power of wind, and how the energy generated can be used to power everything from homes to entire cities.

Another fascinating exhibit is the solar energy installation, which demonstrates how the power of the sun can be harnessed to create electricity. Visitors will be able to see solar panels in action, and learn how they work to generate clean, renewable energy.

In addition to these exhibits, the Energetica exhibition also features a variety of other interactive displays, including a "smart grid" that shows how electricity is transmitted and distributed, and a "power play" area where visitors can experiment with generating electricity using different sources.

Overall, the Energetica exhibition is a must-see for anyone interested in sustainable energy and the future of our planet. Whether you are a student, a teacher, or simply someone with a passion for science and technology, this exhibition is sure to inspire and amaze. So come along and experience the power of the elements for yourself - you won't be disappointed!
Previously held events:
Astronomy Night
The wonders of the cosmos! 24th of January 2023
Last month, the Community Science Museum hosted an exciting event called "Astronomy Night". The event was a huge success, with over 200 people attending to learn about the wonders of the cosmos.

The night started with a presentation by Dr. Lisa Anderson, an expert astronomer from the nearby university. Dr. Anderson gave a fascinating talk about the latest discoveries in astronomy, including the search for habitable planets outside of our solar system, the mysteries of black holes, and the hunt for dark matter. After the presentation, attendees were treated to a guided tour of the museum's observatory, where they were able to view the stars and planets through a high-powered telescope. Expert astronomers were on hand to answer questions and provide insights into what was being seen through the telescope. As the night progressed, attendees were also able to participate in a variety of fun and interactive activities, including a "build your own rocket" workshop, a star-gazing scavenger hunt, and even a virtual reality experience that took them on a journey through the universe.

Overall, Astronomy Night was a fantastic event that inspired and engaged people of all ages with the wonders of the cosmos. The Community Science Museum looks forward to hosting similar events in the future to continue inspiring curiosity and passion for science among its visitors.
Robotic Innovations
Robotics and AI! 16th of June 2022
Last year, the Community Science Museum hosted a fascinating event called "Robotic Innovations". The event was a showcase of the latest developments in robotics and artificial intelligence, and featured presentations, demonstrations, and interactive exhibits from some of the leading researchers and companies in the field.

One of the highlights of the event was a keynote speech by Dr. Mark Johnson, a renowned robotics expert from a nearby university. Dr. Johnson discussed the latest breakthroughs in the field, including advances in machine learning, natural language processing, and computer vision. He also gave a demonstration of a humanoid robot that he and his team had developed, which was able to perform complex tasks such as object recognition and manipulation.

In addition to the keynote speech, there were also a variety of exhibits and demonstrations throughout the museum. Visitors were able to see robots in action, including industrial robots used in manufacturing and healthcare robots used in hospitals. They were also able to try their hand at programming a robot, using a variety of kid-friendly programming tools.

Another popular exhibit was the virtual reality exhibit, where visitors could experience what it was like to control a robot through a virtual environment. They were able to see the robot move and interact with objects in the environment, providing a unique and immersive experience.

Overall, Robotic Innovations was a huge success, attracting visitors from across the region who were interested in learning more about the latest developments in robotics and artificial intelligence. The Community Science Museum is already planning its next event in the series, and is excited to continue inspiring curiosity and innovation in the field of robotics.
